# Replit Prompt: Ethical FOMO + Ownership Framing for Orbit Claiming

## Goal
Increase Orbit claim conversion using ownership psychology and soft FOMO,
without fear-based or manipulative language.

## Rules
- No scare tactics
- No countdown timers
- No “someone else will take it” threats
- Emphasise ownership, legitimacy, and control

## Changes to Implement

### 1) Update CTA Copy
Replace:
"Claim This Orbit"

With:
"Claim your business Orbit"

### 2) Add Microcopy Under CTA (only if unclaimed)
Display one line beneath the button:

"This Orbit is available for the verified owner of {domain}"

or

"Orbits are claimed on a first-come, domain-verified basis."

### 3) Add Claim Confirmation Modal
Before auth flow, show a modal:

Title:
"Claim your business Orbit"

Body:
"This Orbit has been created for {domain}. Claiming it confirms you as the
verified owner and unlocks control over content, branding, and insights.
Once claimed, this Orbit is reserved for your domain."

Primary CTA:
"Verify and claim Orbit"

Secondary:
"Learn more"

### 4) Echo Awareness Nudge (One-Time Only)
If user interacts with Orbit content but has not claimed:

Echo may say once:
"Just so you know, this Orbit is still unclaimed. If this is your business,
claiming it ensures it stays under your control."

Track via:
orbitClaimNudgeShown = true

### 5) Guardrails
- Never imply someone else is actively claiming it
- Never show urgency language more than once
- Never show to already-claimed orbits

## Definition of Done
- Claim CTA feels like ownership, not warning
- Microcopy reinforces legitimacy
- No user reports of pressure or fear
- Conversion increases without drop in trust signals
